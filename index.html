Choosing The Right Booking Platform For Your Property?

Sunny BNB
  • When considering which Online Travel Agent (OTA) to use for your property bookings, Airbnb often comes to mind first. It's a popular choice, especially for short-term listings around specific periods like the Christmas holidays. However, if you're planning to list your property for the long term, it's important to think strategically and consider all your options.
  • While Airbnb, Booking.com, and Stayz (VRBO) are common go-tos, they might not necessarily be the best platforms for every property owner. In fact, we believe that the optimal booking platform is one you create and manage yourself. Owning your booking platform puts you in control, allowing you to avoid the uncertainties and limitations that come with relying solely on external OTAs.

The Risks Of Relying On External Platforms

  • Using platforms like Airbnb or Booking.com introduces what we call 'platform risk.' These platforms have the power to suspend listings, issue refunds to guests without consulting the host, deny claims, delay payments, and raise feesâ€”all without recourse for the property owners. For example, if Airbnb mistakenly freezes your listings due to a misidentified smoke alarm, your entire booking channel could be blocked, leaving you helpless if that's your only booking source.

The Benefits Of Your Own Booking Site

  • By establishing your own booking site, such as we did with sunnycoastbnb.com.au, you can significantly mitigate these risks. From our experience, after Airbnb implemented updates that negatively impacted our listings, we diversified our approach by also listing on Booking.com, Stayz, and our own site. This strategy has not only reduced our dependency on Airbnb but also spread the risk and increased our control over the booking process.
  • Today, Airbnb accounts for 35-48% of all our bookings, a substantial decrease from the previous 100%. This balance allows us to maintain visibility on popular OTAs while building up our direct bookings, which are more cost-effective for both guests and us due to the absence of OTA fees.

Maximizing Bookings Through Multiple Channels

  • It's a good strategy to fish where the fish are, meaning listing your property where potential guests are looking. However, it's equally important to diversify your booking channels. If you can drive 25-50% of your bookings through your own site, you achieve an ideal balance, reducing OTA fees and increasing your direct interactions with guests.
  • We continue to list detailed house rules, verify guest identities, and document any issues at our properties. Despite this diligence, we've faced challenges with OTAs like Airbnb where claims were partially denied or guests were unjustly refunded. These experiences underscore the importance of having multiple channels to mitigate the impact of such decisions.

Conclusion

  • While OTAs play a crucial role in attracting guests, they also carry inherent risks that can affect your business. By listing on multiple platforms and prioritizing your own booking site, you can create a balanced and resilient booking strategy that minimizes risks and maximizes your control over guest interactions and revenues. This approach not only safeguards your interests but also provides a more direct and personalized booking experience for your guests.
